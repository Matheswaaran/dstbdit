The 16th ISTE Annual Student Convention is a forum for exchanging scientific and technical innovative concepts and emphasizing the role of students in converting intelligence with excellence. The convention provides an ambient platform for both academicians and industrialists for a collaborative work and to discuss technical advancements and to promote innovations and invention for conducive lifestyle in the postmodern era. The convention comprises of several technical events to encourage their expertise in knowledge and creativity matching with global needs.
Theme
  The theme of the convention is “Promoting Innovative Practical Ability and Employability Skills in Engineering Education ”.
Subthemes
  – Electronics System Design and Communication for Next Generation
  - Power Electronic Circuits for Green Energy Technologies
  - Technological Revolutions of Social Media
  - Virtual Manufacturing
  - ICT for rural development



Events in the Programme

Paper Presentation

  • Maximum no. of team members is 3.
  • Submission of abstract (in a word document) for not more than 300 words before the due date.
  • Based on the peering review of the abstract, few solutions will be selected for presenting the paper.
  • Each team can choose to solve any one of the problem statements.
  • Only one entry per team is allowed.
  • Participants have to mail their Team Description Papers (TDP) for the preliminary round. The selection for the finals would be based on this.
  • The designs can be altered for the finals but change of problem statement will not be entertained.
  • Stress analysis, flow/thermal analysis, mechanical simulation will be awarded extra points.(Not mandatory)
  • PLAGIARISM, in any way, is strictly prohibited.

Best out of Waste

  • Maximum no. of team members is 3.
  • Participants are suppose to make anything to their creativity from the the waste products given to them.
  • Each team can choose to solve any one of the problem statements.
  • Only one entry per team is allowed.
  • We will be providing them with waste material like cd,dvds, cardboards, sheets, pens, pencils, markers, a box of waste products etc.
  • from which they need to make any 3D model without copying from others.
  • The designs can be altered for the finals but change of materials will not be entertained.
  • PLAGIARISM, in any way, is strictly prohibited.

Photography

  • Maximum no. of team members is 3.
  • Submitted images should be in JPEG format and at least 1,600 pixels wide for a horizontal image or 1,600 pixels tall for a vertical image.
  • All submitted images are non-returnable. The file name for each image should be clearly labelled with the participant’s name, and where the image was taken.
  • Each team can choose to solve any one of the problem statements.
  • Only one entry per team is allowed.
  • Any accepted CAD software can be used. (Not Mandatory) E.g. AutoCAD, Solid works, Catia, ProE, Autodesk inventor, etc.
  • Participants have to mail their Team Description Papers (TDP) for the preliminary round. The selection for the finals would be based on this.
  • The designs can be altered for the finals but change of problem statement will not be entertained.
  • Stress analysis, flow/thermal analysis, mechanical simulation will be awarded extra points.(Not mandatory)
  • PLAGIARISM, in any way, is strictly prohibited.

Technical Quiz

  • Only team entries are eligible.
  • A team shall consist of max two persons.
  • The decision of the quiz-master will be final and will not be subjected to any change.
  • The participants shall not be allowed to use mobile or other electronic instruments.
  • The questions shall be in the form of multiple choice, True / False statement, Specific-answer question etc.
  • Audience shall not give any hints or clues to the competitors.
  • Replacement of any participant of a team is not allowed after registration.
  • Teams selected for the final rounds will be allowed to give themselves an appropriate name related to the competition by which they may want to be known.
